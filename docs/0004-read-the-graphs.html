Read the Graphs

The exam shows you a figure and asks what it is, which part means what, and what belongs in the blank. Rehearse on the real slide graphs.

How to drill this (retrieval, not recognition) Each numbered blue box hides a label. Type the hidden label in the answer sheet under the figure and hit Check (Enter works) — spelling is forgiving, key words are enough. Stuck? Show gives that one up (gold = you owe it a revision). Clicking a box jumps to its input. Prefer the old click-to-peek? Hit Click-reveal mode. After each figure, a “details not hidden in the picture” quiz covers the rest of the slide text — type the answer or press Choices to make it multiple choice.

1 · The Medieval Commercial quadrangle

The classic essay-graph. Four trade circuits linking N. Europe ↔ the Mediterranean. Name each circuit, then recall what flowed along it.

2 · The logistic / life-cycle model of development

An S-curve read as a life cycle. Name the five phases along the top, in order — then the sub-phases and critical points the boxes don’t hide.

3 · World-economic position of a country

A country's place in the world economy has three dimensions — the three axes of this cube. Name each axis.

4 · The two-dimensional map of development

Development is more than money: it has an economic axis and a human-social axis. Study the figure, then answer.

5 · Centre & periphery (world-systems)

Four country groups plotted by size and efficiency. Fix which label sits in which corner.

6 · The nature of chaos

The surprise-question favourite. One tiny equation, four consequences for the global economy. Name each of the four branches — then the small print under them.

7 · The short 20th-century timeline

True/False questions hinge on dates. Rebuild this timeline from memory: the two boundary years, the three era names — then every row of the comparison.

If a figure comes up blank in the exam, reconstruct it from these anchors Quadrangle: Hanseatic (N. Sea/Baltic) · Varangian (rivers to Kiev/Byzantium) · Levante (Med. luxury) · Overland & river (Champagne fairs).
Life-cycle: Initiation → Growth → Maturity → Declension → Stationary/exit.
Country position: size of country · position of companies · state of development.
Chaos (Zt+1=Zt²+c): feed-back loops · simple rule · sensitivity to initial conditions · non-linearity.
Timeline: 1780 –|1919|– short 20th c. –|1989|– global age.
