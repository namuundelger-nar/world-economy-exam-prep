Two terms examiners love Creative destruction (Schumpeter, from Marx): the "perennial gale" — new products/methods/markets arise, un-adaptive firms die, capital & labour reallocate to productive sectors (e.g. streaming killed video rental).
Mode of production (Marx): how a society organises material life = forces of production (tools, tech, labour) + relations of production (class relations over who owns the means of production).

The logistic (S) curve — critical points

PointMeaning
Lower elbow (E)Jump from accelerating growth; new phenomenon becomes visible, proves viable → enters flourishing phase.
Inflection (I)Most important change: deep quality of development shifts; decisive driving forces "weaken." Flourishing → maturity. Either renewed impetus or a new qualitative era.
Upper elbow (F)Slowing growth makes achievable development visibly more limited.
Saturation start (H)Life-curve's 2nd inflection — actual start of decline / run-off.
End of run-off (Z)Function approaches its possible maximum (in the infinite).

3 · Development — concept & measurement

telos = purpose / end eudaimonia = meaning & purpose in life / good psychological functioning objective vs subjective measures
Measurement sideElements & instruments
ObjectiveHard indicators (GDP, HDI, etc. — see §4).
SubjectiveCognitive scaling / satisfaction: Cantril scale, Diener scale.
Positive feelings (≠ emotions!) → Happiness (happiness measures, Beck depression survey, U-index).
Negative feelings: fear, anger. Plus trust + optimism. And eudaimonia.
Name to know Stiglitz–Sen–Fitoussi — the 2009 Commission on the Measurement of Economic Performance and Social Progress (Joseph Stiglitz, Amartya Sen, Jean-Paul Fitoussi): "GDP isn't enough" — measure well-being & sustainability.

5 · Explaining development differences — three schools

Dependency school (1950s; critique of modernisation — Reyes 2001)

Term / nameMeaning
Raúl PrebischECLAC; core–periphery model & Import Substitution (ISI) strategy.
Andre Gunder FrankDevelopment in the core causes underdevelopment in the periphery.
Centre–PeripheryCentre industrialises & gains from high-value production; periphery supplies raw materials, stays underdeveloped.
Asymmetric dependencyPeriphery subordinate to core for tech, capital, markets.
ISIReplace imports with protected domestic production to industrialise.
Immiserating growthGrowth worsens terms of trade & welfare (over-reliance on primary exports).
Unequal exchangeCore–periphery trade is inherently exploitative; value flows poor → rich.

World-systems theory

Term / nameMeaning
Immanuel WallersteinFounder; world as a single integrated capitalist system; long historical cycles.
Fernand BraudelHistorian; longue durée (long-term) approach influenced Wallerstein.
World-systemSocial system with a boundary, structures, groups, rules — spans nation-states.
Semi-peripheryIn-between tier; stabilises the system (prevents bipolar core-vs-periphery conflict).
Single-society assumptionCritique: don't treat nation-states as isolated units — the global system is the unit.

Institutional school (1990+, 2010+)

Term / nameMeaning
Daron AcemogluCo-author Why Nations Fail; institutions are the primary driver of prosperity differences.
Francis FukuyamaState capacity & rule of law essential for institutional strength.
Thorstein VeblenEarly institutional school; "leisure class," conspicuous consumption; culture shapes economics.
InstitutionsHumanly devised constraints — formal (laws, property rights) + informal (norms, customs) — "the rules of the game."
Inclusive vs ExtractiveInclusive: broad participation, protect property, foster innovation. Extractive: wealth from many → narrow elite; stunts growth.
Middle-income trapMiddle-income countries stall before high-income — fail to upgrade institutions & innovation.

6 · Developmental state & oligopolistic structure

Amartya Sen Development as freedom — development = expanding real freedoms people enjoy.

Developmental state: state is not just a regulator but an active orchestrator — coordinates actors, corrects market failures, does long-term strategic planning, provides infrastructure. Economic policy = "organic part of the system," not a distortion.

Oligopolistic structure — three tiers

TierWho
Oligopolistic sectorFew dominant firms whose decisions individually move the national/global economy.
SymbiontsMany small firms that survive off the oligopoly (subcontractors, cast-off tech).
OutsidersFirms outside the structure; less profitable / peak-capacity fillers.
Pricing & rents (constrained factors) Prices set by the upper-threshold cost of 3 constrained factors → stable excess profit = rent: natural resources → production rent, technology/capital → capital rent, novelty/innovation → "brain rent." Only routine labour is unconstrained → enters at its lower threshold (lowest wage).

Chaos equation — Zt+1 = Zt² + c The Mandelbrot set iteration. Zt = current state · Zt+1 = next state · c = constant parameter (the "control"/environment). Small changes → non-linear, unpredictable outcomes (butterfly effect): metaphor for global systems flipping from stability to volatility.
