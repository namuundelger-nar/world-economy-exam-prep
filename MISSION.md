# Mission: Global Economy (exam cram)

## Why
Pass an upcoming Global Economy exam **today** (~3 hours out). The course is
conceptual and interdisciplinary — macrohistory, schools of thought, thinkers,
key terms, and development/measurement concepts. Success = recalling the right
names, terms, and connections fast under exam pressure.

## Success looks like
- Correctly match thinkers → their concept, era, and school (e.g. Ibn Khaldun → cyclical change / asabiyah).
- Recall the meaning of key terms (asabiyah, creative destruction, substantive economy, eudaimonia, etc.) on demand.
- Distinguish the schools / models of change (cyclical vs linear vs spiral; institutional school) and who belongs to each.
- Explain development as a concept and how it is measured (objective vs subjective; Cantril, Diener, eudaimonia).

## Constraints
- **Hard deadline: exam in ~3 hours today (2026-07-01).** Cram mode.
- Preferred formats: compressed tables/cheat-sheets, quiz/recall drills, diagrams/concept maps.
- No time for long-term spacing — optimise for fast in-the-moment retrieval.

## Out of scope
- Deep theory, primary-source reading, anything not plausibly on the exam.
- Long-term retention strategy (revisit after the exam if desired).
